Alternative Breaks (UM)

Hours: 32

  • On my second Alternative Breaks trip I volunteered with Habitat for Humanity Restore. This was in Martin County, Florida from October 11, 2025, to October 14, 2025. More information about Alternative Breaks is available on the clubs page. On this trip, I was a site leader. This means I spoke for the group, gave the group direction, and helped create plans for how we went about each day. During our trip, the Habitat for Humanity Restore was moving to a new location. We spent the whole trip helping with their move.
    On day one, we helped tear down old furniture in the new location to make room for the new furniture. This included breaking cabinets and dumping rubble. On day one, I broke my nail, and it hurt really badly. I did not let that impact my availability to help throughout the trip.
    On day two, Habitat for Humanity Restore was closed. On this day off, the group did a beach cleanup.
    On day three, we helped to load many boxes onto a moving truck from the old location. This was a great team effort since the boxes were heavy and plentiful. We split into two groups. One group was loading boxes onto a dolly, and the other group was pushing the dolly and loading the boxes onto the moving truck. Later that same day, we went to a sea turtle hospital called Loggerhead Marinelife Center. This was thanks to one of my group members who works there, Caitlin.
    On day four, we helped to unload the moving truck full of boxes to the new location. This was also a team effort. It required us to split into two groups again. One group had people loading the boxes onto a dolly, and another group pushing the dolly and unloading the boxes in the new location.

    Throughout this experience, my group learned a lot about each other. This was most of my group's first Alternative Breaks trip. I got to tell them about my experience on my previous trip. I also learned about where the other members are from and what they aspire to do in the future. Working in groups meant that the hard work was not only done more efficiently, but more fun as well. Besides the group members, we also met great people in Martin County. The construction manager, Jeff, was so kind throughout our trip. When I hurt my nail, he took great care to ensure that I was okay. I spoke with many employees, and they all had unique stories to tell about themselves. This trip taught me a lot in such a short amount of time. I learned leadership as a site leader. I learned independence after hurting myself while so far away from home and relying on myself to remain composed and collected as a site leader. I learned to have confidence when speaking for a group and when speaking to new people. I also learned that there is inherent value in branching out and meeting new people. Meeting Caitlin made it possible to visit the sea turtle hospital.

    • Alternative Breaks (FIU)

    Hours: 49

  • Being a part of Alternative Breaks, I had the privilege to go on a weekend trip to volunteer in Tampa. More information about Alternative Breaks is available on the clubs page. On this trip we went to Tampa, Florida from December 15, 2024, to December 18, 2024. On the 15th, we explored Tampa. On the 16th, we volunteered with The Samaritans Purse. We helped make a woman's house livable after being devastated by Hurricane Milton. We did this by reinforcing windows to prevent water from getting into her home. I learned how to use some tools and equipment for home repair that I had never used before. This experience helped me learn that if I am more fortunate than others, it does not take a lot of effort to help others who are not as fortunate. On the 17th, we volunteered with The Salvation Army. We helped prepare gifts for underprivileged families. We did this by sorting clothing for children by age and gender. We would try to match clothing and other items to what the people had asked for. This experience helped me learn that I do not have to spend any money just to help people who do not have disposable cash. Spending money holds people back from helping their community, but it does not have to cost a single cent.
